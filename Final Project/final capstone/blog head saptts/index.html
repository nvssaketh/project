Introduction

 

Smart farming, also known as precision

agriculture, is transforming the agriculture

industry. By integrating advanced techno-

logies like IoT, AI, drones, and big data,

farmers can increase efficiency, reduce

waste, and boost the key components and

benefits os smart farming.

 

Key Components of Smart farming :-

 

  1. Internet of Things(IoT)

 

 

Sensors : Soil moisture, temperature, and

nutrient sensor help monitor field condition

in real-time.

 

Devices : Connected devices like smart

tractors and irrigation system enable

automated farming processes.

 

  1. Artificial Intelligence (AI)

 

Predictive Analysis : AI algorithms analyze

weather pattern, crop health, and market

trends to guide farming decision.

 

Automation : Robots and AI-driven machi-

nery handle planting, harvesting, and sort-

ing, reducing labor cost.

 

  1. Drones And Satellites

 

Aerial Imaging : Drones and Satellites

provide high-resolution images of fields,

helping identity issues like pest infestation

or nutrient deficiencies.

 

Mapping : These technology create details

maps for precision farming, ensuring opti-

mal resource allocation .

 

  1. Big Data

 

Data collection : Collection data from vari-

ous source(weather stations, sensor, mar-

kets data) allow for comprehensive analysis

 

Decision Making : Big data analytics

support informed decision-making

enhancing productivity and sustainability.
